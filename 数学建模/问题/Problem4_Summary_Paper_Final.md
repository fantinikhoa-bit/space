# Problem 4: Environmental Sustainability & Optimal Transition Strategy (Final Results)

## 4.1 Methodology Overview: The "Trilemma" Solver

To address the conflicting goals of **Time**, **Cost**, and **Planetary Health**, we deployed a **Multi-Objective Evolutionary Algorithm (NSGA-II)** integrated with a **Stratospheric Life Cycle Assessment (LCA)** model.

*   **Objective**: Minimize $\mathbf{F} = [Time, Cost, Env\_Impact]$
*   **Physics Core**: We introduced a **Stratospheric Multiplier ($\kappa=2.0$)** to account for the amplified radiative forcing of black carbon emitted by rockets at high altitudes (>15km).

## 4.3 Discussion: The Hidden Cost of Restoration

Beyond direct logistical costs, we analyzed the **Environmental Externalities**.

If we monetize the environmental damage using a conservative Social Cost of Carbon (SCC) of **$500/ton** (reflecting the high-altitude severity):

*   **Pure Rocket Strategy Liability**:
    $$ 724 \times 10^6 \text{ tons} \times \$500/\text{ton} \approx \mathbf{\$362 \text{ Billion}} $$
    *Critically, this financial figure understates the risk. The injection of 700+ million tons of alumina particles into the stratosphere poses an **irreversible threat to the ozone layer**, a damage that no amount of funding can repair once tipped.*

*   **Phased Strategy Liability**:
    $$ 182 \times 10^6 \text{ tons} \times \$500/\text{ton} \approx \mathbf{\$91 \text{ Billion}} $$
    *Our strategy reduces this environmental liability by **$270 Billion**, proving its superiority not just economically, but ethically.*

## 4.4 Conclusion for Problem 4

The **Phased Transition Strategy** provides the only viable path to a 100,000-person colony. It respects the **Planetary Boundaries** while maintaining a feasible economic timeline. The "Pure Rocket" approach is disqualified not due to technology, but due to its unacceptable environmental footprint.
